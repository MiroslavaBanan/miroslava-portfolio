As a volunteer tutor, I worked with two students for about eight months. We had around 31 one‑hour lessons in total. Despite frequent schedule changes due to school and health, both girls built a strong English foundation and improved their school performance.

I focused on basics, consistency, and independent learning. I taught them how to manage their time, find materials, and study without relying fully on a tutor. My goal was to give them confidence and show that English can be a useful tool, not just a school subject.

Programming Instructor - IT Basics for Kids

Period: 2 monthes | September-November 2024
Students: 3 groups, 15-20 per group
Format: Offline, group classes
Skills:
Lesson Planning
Communication
Time Management
Building Learning Strategies
Classroom Management
Link to the institution

I taught three groups of children aged 7-9, around 15-20 students in each group. We covered basic computer literacy, hardware components, and simple tools like Word and Excel. Later, we created small animations in Pencil2D.

I planned all lessons myself, added short breaks, and came up with small games to help them relax and stay focused. I also introduced them to different IT fields and tried to spark curiosity. This experience helped me learn how to communicate clearly, stay patient, and adapt explanations for young students.

Independent Web Development School - Mentor

Period: 6-7 months | 2023-2024
Students: ~15 graduates
Format: Online, group classes
Skills:
Mentoring
Curriculum Design
Web Development Teaching
Code Review
Community Building
Leadership
Consistency

Together with two friends, I helped create a small free school for beginner developers. I taught web development from scratch to people my age and older. Around 15 students completed the program with strong results.

We held classes three times a week for an hour, kept the pace manageable, checked homework, and set deadlines. The idea was to build a small community of people who could grow together and work on shared projects. This experience taught me how to mentor adults, structure material, and support students through the learning process.
