The Lembo Glamping website was created for a small hospitality business near St. Petersburg that offers short-term cabin rentals. The company needed a clear and reliable online presence to present their services, manage bookings, and support daily operations.

I designed and developed the website using WordPress, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. A key part of the work was integrating a third-party booking system and structuring the content so it could be easily managed by non-technical staff. During development, project requirements evolved, and I created several prototypes to help stakeholders compare options and make informed decisions.

The website was launched within one month and has been actively used for more than a year. I continued to support the project after launch by handling updates, SEO improvements, and technical issues, as well as preparing short guidelines for the team. This project taught me how to work with changing requirements, communicate with non-technical users, and deliver a stable product in a real business environment.

This website was created for a small private transportation service operating routes between Poland and Kaliningrad. The goal was to design a simple one-page site that stands out among competitors and clearly presents key information without unnecessary navigation.

I developed the website within approximately one week using WordPress, with additional HTML and CSS for interactive elements and custom layout details. The site was adapted for all device types and included basic performance optimization and SEO setup. The focus was on clear structure, typography, and visual clarity.

Through this project, I gained experience working with small businesses, experimenting with design solutions, and delivering a complete product within a very short timeframe.

Contrast Shop&Lounge was created as a promotional landing page for a local lounge and retail partner associated with the Hookah Time brand. The task was to design and develop the site within a very short timeframe, while maintaining visual consistency with the original brand and clearly communicating that the website represented a partner rather than the official brand platform.

I focused on visual identity, layout, and clear differentiation between brand reference and independent presentation. The project required balancing recognizability with originality, as well as prioritizing essential content due to strict time constraints.

This experience strengthened my ability to work with existing brand guidelines, design under tight deadlines, and make clear design decisions in a commercial context.
